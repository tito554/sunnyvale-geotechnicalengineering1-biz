We still see projects in Sunnyvale where the initial site class is assumed from a regional map without verifying the 30-meter shear wave velocity profile. That shortcut backfires fast here. The city sits on Quaternary alluvium from the Santa Clara Valley aquifer system, but the transition from stiff alluvial clays near Baylands Park to looser deposits south of Highway 237 can shift the site class from D to E within a single parcel. Our laboratory runs the full seismic microzonation sequence: downhole seismic, MASW, and crosshole surveys processed against ASTM D1586 borehole data, so the ground motion parameters fed into the structural model actually match the 37.37°N coordinates of your site. When the USGS updates its Bay Area seismic hazard curves, having a site-specific Vs30 instead of a proxy value keeps the design spectrum defensible under Sunnyvale's plan check review.

A site-specific Vs30 profile in Sunnyvale often reveals a 15 to 20 percent stiffer response than the USGS proxy, directly reducing the seismic base shear in the structural design.

Methodology and scope

The microclimate contrast between the bayfront humidity and the drier inland corridor east of Mathilda Avenue affects our field acquisition windows differently depending on the season. In the winter months, elevated groundwater levels in Sunnyvale’s shallow aquifer saturate the near-surface silts, which complicates shear wave arrival picks unless we couple the geophones with a grouted borehole installation. We combine the downhole seismic array with a MASW surface wave survey for dispersion curve redundancy, then calibrate the velocity profile against SPT blow counts from the same boring to satisfy the site classification requirements of ASCE 7-22 Chapter 20. Our lab technicians run the full processing chain—picking first arrivals, inverting the dispersion curve, and computing the travel-time interval velocities—on the same day as the field acquisition so that any data gaps are identified before demobilizing the rig.

Site-specific factors

The risk profile between a project near the Sunnyvale Water Pollution Control Plant and one in the Cherry Chase neighborhood illustrates why microzonation matters here. The bayfront area sits on younger Holocene bay mud with lower shear wave velocities and a higher liquefaction susceptibility index. The inland residential zone, sitting on older alluvial fan deposits, typically classifies as Site Class D with a shorter fundamental period. If both projects use the same default site class from a county-wide map, the bayfront structure ends up with an unconservative design spectrum that underestimates the long-period spectral acceleration. The IBC explicitly permits site-specific ground motion analysis to refine the mapped values, and the City of Sunnyvale’s building division expects that refinement on any structure over three stories or with an irregular lateral system.

Complementary services

01

Downhole Seismic Velocity Profiling

ASTM D7400-compliant downhole survey with a triaxial geophone array lowered in a grouted, cased borehole. Interval velocities computed every 1.5 meters, with first-arrival picks verified by reverse polarity shot stacking.

02

MASW Surface Wave Survey

Multichannel Analysis of Surface Waves with a 24-channel land streamer, providing a 2D Vs cross-section. The dispersion curve inversion is constrained by the downhole velocity profile to resolve low-velocity layers.

03

Site-Specific Response Spectrum

Ground motion amplification factors Fa and Fv computed from the measured Vs30 and the USGS NSHM hazard curves. The report includes the design response spectrum for the MCER and design earthquake levels per ASCE 7-22.

04

Liquefaction Triggering Analysis

Cyclic stress ratio evaluation using SPT blow counts corrected for fines content and overburden. The factor of safety against liquefaction is mapped across the depth profile for the Sunnyvale groundwater conditions.

Questions and answers

When does the City of Sunnyvale require a site-specific seismic microzonation study?

The building division follows the IBC 2024 requirements: any structure assigned to Seismic Design Category D, E, or F with a fundamental period greater than 0.5 seconds typically triggers the requirement. Additionally, sites within 5 km of a known fault or with documented soft clay layers thicker than 3 meters will be flagged during plan check, and the reviewer will request a site-specific Vs30 determination rather than accepting a mapped proxy value.

How does the downhole seismic test differ from a standard SPT boring?

The SPT boring provides a disturbed sample and a blow count (N-value) at discrete 1.5-meter intervals, which gives an index of penetration resistance. The downhole seismic test measures the actual travel time of a shear wave generated at the surface and received by a triaxial geophone clamped at successive depths in the same borehole. The interval velocity computed from those travel times is a direct measurement of small-strain stiffness, not a correlation, which is why ASCE 7-22 favors it for site classification.

What is the typical cost range for a seismic microzonation study in Sunnyvale?

A complete microzonation package—including a 30-meter downhole seismic survey, MASW surface wave line, SPT borehole logging, and the final response spectrum report—generally falls in the range of US$4,100 to US$14,800. The variation depends on the total linear footage of the borehole, the number of MASW lines required, and whether the site is in a high-traffic area requiring nighttime lane closures for the surface survey.

How long does the field acquisition and reporting take for a typical Sunnyvale site?

Fieldwork usually takes one to two days: one day for the SPT boring and downhole seismic survey, and a second day for the MASW line if a separate crew is not available. The laboratory data processing, dispersion curve inversion, and report drafting require an additional five to seven business days. Expedited reporting can be arranged if the structural peer review deadline is approaching.

Can the microzonation results reduce the seismic design forces for our Sunnyvale project?

Yes, and this is one of the main reasons project owners commission the study. When the measured Vs30 is higher than the default 259 m/s often assumed for Site Class D in Santa Clara County, the site-specific amplification factors Fa and Fv decrease proportionally. A reduction of 10 to 15 percent in the spectral acceleration at 0.2 seconds is common for Sunnyvale sites with competent alluvial deposits, which translates directly into lower base shear demands and more efficient lateral system design.
